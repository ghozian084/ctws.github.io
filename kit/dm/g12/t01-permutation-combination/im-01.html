📝 Step-by-Step Example

Problem: In how many ways can 5 books be arranged on a shelf?

Step 1: Identify that all 5 objects are being arranged. This is $5!$ (5 factorial).
Step 2: Expand the factorial: $5 \times 4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1$
Step 3: Calculate: $120$ ways.

📝 Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Electing a President, VP, and Secretary from 6 students.

Step 1: Identify $n = 6$ (total students) and $r = 3$ (positions). Order matters, so use $^nP_r$.
Step 2: Formula: $^6P_3 = \frac{6!}{(6-3)!} = \frac{6!}{3!}$
Step 3: Expand and cancel: $\frac{6 \times 5 \times 4 \times 3!}{3!}$
Step 4: Calculate: $6 \times 5 \times 4 = 120$ ways.

📝 Step-by-Step Example

Problem: How many arrangements of the word "KATAK"?

Step 1: Total letters $n = 5$. Total initial arrangements = $5!$.
Step 2: Identify repeating letters. 'K' repeats $2$ times. 'A' repeats $2$ times.
Step 3: Divide out duplicates: $\frac{5!}{2! \times 2!}$
Step 4: Calculate: $\frac{120}{2 \times 2} = \frac{120}{4} = 30$ ways.

📝 Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Choose a debate team of 3 students from a group of 8.

Step 1: Identify $n = 8$ and $r = 3$. Order doesn't matter (it's a team), so use $^nC_r$.
Step 2: Formula: $^8C_3 = \frac{8!}{3!(8-3)!} = \frac{8!}{3!5!}$
Step 3: Expand and cancel: $\frac{8 \times 7 \times 6 \times 5!}{3 \times 2 \times 1 \times 5!}$
Step 4: Calculate: $\frac{8 \times 7 \times 6}{6} = 56$ teams.
