Lesson Plan: Making Sense of Algebra

School: SMP Semesta 2026/2025 | Grade: 9

Time Allocation: 9 Meetings (2 x 40 minutes = 80 mins per meeting)

📌 Topic: 2. Making sense of algebra

Reference: Cambridge IGCSE® Math Core & Extended

Method: 5E, Math Modeling Task Design

Approach: Deep Learning & Culturally STEM

Character Focus: Critical Thinking, Creativity, Self-Regulated, Collaboration

Differentiation: Extension, Scaffolding, EAL, SEN

🎯 Objectives

  • C2.1, E2.1: Know that letters can be used to represent generalised numbers. Substitute numbers into expressions and formulas.
  • C2.2, E2.2: Simplify expressions by collecting like terms. Expand products of algebraic expressions.
  • C1.7, E1.7: Understand and use indices (positive, zero, negative, and fractional).
  • C2.4, E2.4: Understand and use the rules of indices.

📅 Meeting 1: Introduction to Letters as Unknowns (Subtopic 2.1)

E1 ENGAGE | Hook & Conceptual Setup [10 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Display a picture of a traditional market (Pasar Bandeng Ungaran).
  • Ask: "If I buy a closed box of Bandeng Presto, I don't know exactly how many fish are inside. If I buy 3 identical boxes, how can I mathematically write down what I have without opening them?"

Introduce Variable Concept (5 min):

  • Hold up a physical blank box. Write an '$x$' on it. Say: "x represents an unknown quantity. If I hold 3 boxes, I have $3x$."
E2 EXPLORE | Math Modeling Intro [20 minutes]
Collaborative Group Work
(Student-led)

Modeling Task 1 (20 min):

  • Distribute physical envelopes (labeled '$y$') containing paper clips, and loose paper clips to groups.
  • Groups must write an algebraic expression for different scenarios: "You have 2 envelopes and 4 loose clips" $\rightarrow 2y + 4$.
  • Collaboration: Students challenge other groups by building physical arrangements and making them write the expression.
  • Joyful Learning: "Guess My Expression" game.
E3 EXPLAIN | Formalizing Expressions [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–5 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Vocabulary: Variable, Coefficient, Term, Constant, Expression.
  • Slide 3: Rule of Algebra: We don't write the multiplication sign. $4 \times a = 4a$. Explain *why* (it looks like an $x$).
  • Slide 4: Word to Algebra translations. "5 more than $y$" $\rightarrow y + 5$.
  • Mindful Check: "Is $x - 5$ the same as $5 - x$? Test it with real numbers on your whiteboard."
Scaffold students: Provide SEN students with an "Algebra Translation Dictionary" mapping phrases like 'less than' to the minus sign.
E4 ELABORATE | Contextual Translation [20 minutes]
Independent Practice
(Student-paced)

Application Task (20 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 2.1 A. Translating English sentences about a local Angkot (public minivan) route into algebraic expressions (e.g., Let $p$ be passengers. At the first stop, 3 get off: $p - 3$).
E5 EVALUATE | Exit Ticket [10 minutes]
Individual Task
(Teacher-monitored)

🎟️ Exit Ticket — Slide 6 (10 min):

  • Write an algebraic expression for: "Double a number $m$, then subtract 7."
  • Hand to the teacher upon exiting.

📅 Meeting 2: Mathematical Modeling with Formulas (Subtopic 2.1)

E1 ENGAGE | Hook & Model Formulation [15 minutes]
Class Discussion
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Show an image of traditional Joglo houses. Ask: "If a builder wants to make a formula to calculate the cost of building $n$ houses, what variables (unknowns) do they need to consider?" (Wood, tiles, labor).

Math Modeling Step 1: Formulate (10 min):

  • Brainstorm variables on the board. Let $W$ = cost of wood, $T$ = cost of tiles. Total Cost $C = W + T$.
E2 EXPLORE | Creating Formulas [25 minutes]
Small Group Work
(Student-led)

Math Modeling Step 2: Develop the Model (25 min):

  • Task: "You are organizing a school trip to Lawang Sewu. The bus costs Rp 500,000 flat, and entry is Rp 10,000 per student. Create a mathematical model (formula) for the Total Cost ($C$) for $n$ students."
  • Critical Thinking: Groups must identify the constant (bus) and the variable cost (tickets).
  • Write models on large paper and stick to the walls.
E3 EXPLAIN | Validating the Model [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Review the groups' models. Correct answer: $C = 500000 + 10000n$.
  • Slide 3: Math Modeling Step 3: Validate. Meaningful connection: Ask: "What if $n = -5$? Does our formula still work mathematically? Yes. Does it make sense in reality? No, you can't have negative students."
  • Explain constraints in real-world mathematical modeling.
Scaffold students: Guide EAL students to map the words "flat rate" to a Constant, and "per person" to a Coefficient.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [25 minutes]
Independent Task
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (15 min):

  • Students create their own real-world scenario and write the corresponding algebraic formula.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • If a taxi charges Rp 15,000 to start and Rp 4,000 per km ($k$), write the formula for the cost ($C$).

📅 Meeting 3: Substitution (Subtopic 2.2)

E1 ENGAGE | Contextual Hook [10 minutes]
Interactive Q&A
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • "In football, a 'substitution' means replacing one player with another. In algebra, it means exactly the same thing: replacing a letter with a number!"

Quick Check (5 min):

  • If $x$ is 5, what is $x + 2$? (7). If $x$ is 5, what is $3x$? (Remind students $3x$ means $3 \times x$, not 35).
E2 EXPLORE | Testing the Model [15 minutes]
Pair Work
(Student-led)

Math Modeling Step 4: Solve/Use the Model (15 min):

  • Return to the Lawang Sewu formula: $C = 500000 + 10000n$.
  • Pairs must calculate the cost if 10, 25, and 40 students go on the trip by substituting $n$.
E3 EXPLAIN | The Bracket Rule [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–5 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Substitution layout. Model writing the equation, replacing the variable using brackets: $3(5) + 4 = 19$.
  • Slide 3: Mindful Substitution: What if we substitute negative numbers? Evaluate $x^2 + 2x$ when $x = -3$.
  • Slide 4: Show common error: $-3^2$ vs $(-3)^2$. Stress that brackets MUST be used when substituting negatives!
Scaffold students: Extension students evaluate complex formulas like the Quadratic Formula or Kinematics $v = u + at$ with mixed signs.
E4 & E5 | Independent Practice & Exit [35 minutes]
Independent Task
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (25 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 2.2 on Substitution.
  • Self-Regulated: Check answers using BODMAS rules explicitly.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • If $a = 4$ and $b = -2$, evaluate: $3a - b^2$.

📅 Meeting 4: Simplifying Expressions (Collecting Like Terms) (Subtopic 2.3)

E1 ENGAGE | Visual Metaphor [10 minutes]
Class Debate
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Show a recipe for Nasi Goreng: 3 eggs ($3e$) and 2 cups of rice ($2r$).
  • Ask: "Can I add $3e + 2r$ and say I have 5 egg-rices ($5er$)?"
  • Meaningful: Establish that you can only combine items that are exactly the same (Like Terms).
E2 EXPLORE | Algebra Tiles [20 minutes]
Collaborative
(Student-led)

Algebra Tiles Activity (20 min):

  • Hand out Algebra Tiles (different shapes for $x^2$, $x$, and units $1$).
  • Write an expression on the board: $2x^2 + 3x + 4 + x^2 - x - 2$.
  • Groups physically group the identical shapes together and count the result.
  • Joyful: "Speed sort" competition.
E3 EXPLAIN | Rules of Like Terms [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Define "Like Terms" (Same letter, exactly the same power). $x$ and $x^2$ are NOT like terms.
  • Slide 3: The sign belongs to the term on its right. Use colored markers to circle terms WITH their signs: $(+4x)$ $(-2y)$ $(-x)$.
  • Slide 4: Model simplifying $5a - 2b + 3a + 5b$. Answer: $8a + 3b$.
Scaffold students: Low achievers use highlighters to color-code like terms before adding/subtracting them.
E4 & E5 | Practice & Evaluation [30 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (20 min):

  • Worksheet 2.3. Students must simplify geometric perimeters (e.g., a triangle with sides $2x$, $x+3$, $3x-1$).

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Simplify: $4x^2 - 3x + 5 - x^2 + 7x$.

📅 Meeting 5: Working with Single Brackets (Subtopic 2.4)

E1 & E2 | Hook & The Area Model [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Pair Work

🪝 Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • "A farmer has a Sawah (rice paddy) with length 5m and width $x$. He buys an extra 3m of land. How do we find the new area?"

Explore — Area Model (20 min):

  • Draw a rectangle split into two parts: one $5 \times x$, one $5 \times 3$.
  • Students realize Total Area = $5x + 15$.
  • Teacher shows this is the same as $5(x + 3)$. This is Expansion!
E3 EXPLAIN | The Distributive Law [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 2-5 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Define Expansion (multiplying the outside term by EVERYTHING inside).
  • Slide 3: Draw the "rainbow" arrows. Example: $-2(3x - 4)$.
  • Slide 4: Mindful Check: Emphasize that multiplying a negative by a negative makes a positive! $-2 \times -4 = +8$.
  • Slide 5: Expanding and then collecting like terms: $3(x + 2) + 2(x - 1)$.
Scaffold students: EAL explicitly teach "Expand" = "Remove Brackets". SEN students keep drawing the Area Model boxes.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min):

  • Worksheet 2.4A. Includes multiplying algebraic terms $x(x + 5) = x^2 + 5x$.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Expand and simplify: $4(y - 2) - 3(y + 1)$.

📅 Meeting 6: Expanding Double Brackets (Subtopic 2.4)

E1 & E2 | 2D Expansion [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Pair Work

🪝 Hook & Explore (25 min):

  • "What happens if BOTH sides of the Sawah are unknown and expanded?" $(x + 3)(x + 2)$.
  • Students draw a 2x2 grid (Box method) to find the 4 separate areas: $x^2$, $3x$, $2x$, $6$.
  • Combine like terms to get the final trinomial.
E3 EXPLAIN | FOIL Method vs Box Method [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 1-4 (20 min):

  • Slide 1: Formalize the Box Method.
  • Slide 2: Introduce FOIL (First, Outer, Inner, Last) as an alternative.
  • Slide 3: Watch out for $(x - 5)^2$. Mindful: It is NOT $x^2 - 25$. It means $(x-5)(x-5)$.
Scaffold students: Extension students expand triple brackets $(x+1)(x+2)(x+3)$. Low achievers stick exclusively to the visual Box method.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Worksheet 2.4B on expanding binomials.
  • Self-Regulated: Students verify their expansion by substituting $x=2$ into the original brackets and their final answer to see if they match.
  • Exit Ticket: Expand and simplify $(2x - 3)(x + 4)$.

📅 Meeting 7: Introduction to Indices (Positive, Zero, Negative, Fractional) (Subtopic 2.5)

E1 & E2 | Pattern Recognition [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Group Work

🪝 Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Population growth of Java island can be modeled using powers of 10. $10^2 = 100$. But what does $10^0$ or $10^{-1}$ physically mean?

Explore the Pattern (20 min):

  • Write on board: $2^3 = 8$, $2^2 = 4$, $2^1 = 2$.
  • Ask groups to figure out the pattern (divide by 2 moving down).
  • Students must confidently predict the value of $2^0$, $2^{-1}$, and $2^{-2}$ based ONLY on the division pattern.
E3 EXPLAIN | Index Definitions [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 2-4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Zero Index Rule: Anything to the power of $0 = 1$.
  • Slide 3: Negative Index Rule: $x^{-n} = 1 / x^n$. Emphasize it means "fraction", not a negative number!
  • Slide 4: Fractional Indices: $x^{1/2} = \sqrt{x}$. Explain the connection to areas and roots from Chapter 1.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Calculate exact values: $5^0$, $3^{-2}$, $16^{1/2}$.
  • Exit Ticket: Evaluate $4^{-2}$ and $25^{1/2}$.

📅 Meeting 8: Rules of Indices (Subtopic 2.5)

E1 & E2 | Deriving the Laws [25 minutes]
Guided Discovery

🪝 Hook & Explore (25 min):

  • "Sound waves of a Gamelan overlapping multiply frequencies."
  • Write: $x^2 \times x^3 = (x \cdot x) \times (x \cdot x \cdot x)$.
  • Ask students to count the x's. "So $x^2 \times x^3 = x^5$. What is the shortcut?" (Add the powers).
  • Have students derive the division rule (subtract) and power-of-a-power rule (multiply) by expanding them out manually first.
E3 EXPLAIN | Formalizing the 3 Laws [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 1-4 (20 min):

  • Slide 1: Multiplication Law: $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$.
  • Slide 2: Division Law: $a^m / a^n = a^{m-n}$.
  • Slide 3: Power Law: $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$.
  • Slide 4: Combining coefficients and indices: $(3x^2)^3 = 27x^6$. Mindful: Don't forget to apply the power to the big number too!
Scaffold students: Extension students combine rules with fractional and negative indices $ \frac{x^3 \times x^{-5}}{x^{1/2}} $.
E4 & E5 | Mastery Practice [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Worksheet 2.5 on Index Laws.
  • Exit Ticket: Simplify $\frac{4x^5 \times 3x^2}{6x^4}$.

📅 Meeting 9: Chapter Review & Summative Evaluation

E1 & E2 | Collaborative Review [20 minutes]
Student-led Review

🪝 Concept Mapping (20 min):

  • Groups are given A3 paper and asked to draw a mind-map connecting: Substitution, Expansion, Like Terms, and Indices.
  • Teacher circulates to clear up any final misconceptions before the test.
E4 & E5 | Chapter Evaluation & Closure [60 minutes]
Summative Test
(Teacher-monitored)

Chapter 2 Test (50 min):

  • Remove visual aids. Distribute the Chapter 2 Evaluation.
  • Self-Regulated: Students pace themselves under strict exam conditions.

Closure & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Self-Reflection: On the back of the test, write one algebraic rule you still find confusing, and one you are an expert at.
🌟 Differentiation Strategies
Profile Strategies & Actions in Class
Extension (High Achievers) Provide complex geometric proofs requiring expansion of binomials (e.g., proving area of composite shapes). Use fractional and negative indices in algebraic fractions. Challenge them to evaluate complex substitution formulas like kinematics ($v = u + at$) with mixed signs.
Scaffolding (Middle/Low) Provide "Algebra Tiles" and physical manipulatives for visual representation of collecting like terms. Give a step-by-step checklist card for FOIL expansion and the Box Method. Guide step-by-step for the first 3 questions of every worksheet.
EAL (English Additional Language) Provide a bilingual glossary of terms: Variable (Variabel), Coefficient (Koefisien), Expand (Jabarkan), Substitute (Substitusi). Encourage visual mapping and use color-coding to link terms with their signs.
SEN (Special Educational Needs) Use physical manipulatives (envelopes and clips) to represent unknowns. Reduce cognitive load by keeping expressions to single variables initially. Use high-contrast printouts and increase spacing between questions on worksheets.

📝 Worksheets Compilation (For Practice & Homework)

Worksheet 2.1 & 2.2: Math Modeling & Substitution

1. Write an expression for: "Multiply $x$ by 4, then subtract 9."

2. A taxi costs Rp 10,000 base fare plus Rp 5,000 per kilometer ($k$). Write a formula for Cost ($C$).

3. If $a = 3$, $b = -2$, and $c = 5$, evaluate:
i) $4a - b$
ii) $b^2 + 2c$
iii) $\frac{a + c}{b}$

Worksheet 2.3 & 2.4: Simplifying & Brackets

1. Simplify by collecting like terms: $5x^2 - 3x + 2 + 2x^2 + 7x - 4$.

2. Expand and simplify: $3(2x - 5) - 2(x + 4)$.

3. Expand and simplify the double brackets: $(x + 5)(x - 3)$.

4. Expand and simplify: $(2x - 1)^2$.

Worksheet 2.5: Rules of Indices

1. Evaluate the exact values: a) $7^0$    b) $5^{-2}$    c) $64^{1/2}$    d) $8^{1/3}$

2. Simplify using index laws:
i) $y^4 \times y^6$
ii) $\frac{m^8}{m^3}$
iii) $(p^3)^4$
iv) $2x^3 \times 4x^5$

💯 Chapter 2 Evaluation (Used in Meeting 9)

Instructions: Answer all questions. Show all working steps clearly. Total: 35 Marks.

Part A: Modeling & Substitution (10 Marks)

1. Write an algebraic expression for "the sum of $x$ and $y$, divided by 3". (2 marks)

2. The formula for converting Celsius to Fahrenheit is $F = \frac{9}{5}C + 32$. Find $F$ when $C = 20$. (3 marks)

3. Given $p = -4$ and $q = 3$, calculate the value of $p^2 - 2q$. (5 marks)

Part B: Algebra Operations (15 Marks)

4. Simplify fully: $4xy - 2x^2 + 3yx + 5x^2$. (3 marks)

5. Expand and simplify: $5(3a - 2b) - 2(a - 4b)$. (5 marks)

6. Expand and simplify: $(3x + 2)(x - 5)$. (4 marks)

7. The length of a rectangle is $(x+4)$ and its width is $(x-1)$. Find an expression for its Area, expanded and simplified. (3 marks)

Part C: Indices (10 Marks)

8. Find the exact numerical value of $36^{1/2} + 2^{-3}$. (4 marks)

9. Simplify fully: $\frac{12a^6b^3}{4a^2b^5}$. Write your answer using positive indices. (3 marks)

10. Simplify fully: $(2x^4)^3$. (3 marks)
