Lesson Plan: Collecting, Organising and Displaying Data

School: SMP Semesta 2026/2025 | Grade: 9

Time Allocation: 5 Meetings (2 x 40 minutes = 80 mins per meeting)

📌 Topic: 4. Data Handling

Reference: Cambridge IGCSE® Math Core & Extended

Method: Problem-Based (PBL) & Project-Based Learning (PjBL)

Approach: Deep Learning & Hands-on Activity

Character Focus: Critical Thinking, Creativity, Self-Regulated, Collaboration

Differentiation: Extension, Scaffolding, EAL, SEN

🎯 Objectives

  • C9.1, E9.1: Classify and tabulate statistical data (Primary/Secondary, Qualitative/Quantitative, Discrete/Continuous).
  • C9.4, E9.4: Draw and interpret statistical charts and diagrams.
  • C9.4, E9.4: Draw and interpret bar charts, pie charts, pictograms, stem-and-leaf diagrams, simple frequency distributions.

📅 Meeting 1: Collecting & Classifying Data (Subtopic 4.1) [PBL Approach]

PBL Phase 1 | Orienting Students to the Problem [15 minutes]
Class Discussion
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Display a picture of overflowing trash bins in the school cafeteria filled with untouched vegetables.
  • "Our cafeteria is wasting money and food because they don't know what students actually like to eat. How can we solve this mathematically?"

Problem Introduction (10 min):

  • Meaningful: Explain that to solve real-world problems, we need Data.
  • Discuss Primary vs Secondary data. "If we ask students directly, is that primary or secondary?"
PBL Phase 2 | Organizing for Study (Concepts) [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Data Types. Qualitative (Words) vs Quantitative (Numbers).
  • Slide 3: Quantitative breakdown: Discrete (counted, exact) vs Continuous (measured, can be decimals).
  • Slide 4: Quick Fire Check: "Shoe size?" (Discrete). "Height?" (Continuous). "Favorite Color?" (Qualitative).
Scaffold students: Provide EAL students with a visual chart showing "Discrete = Counting objects" and "Continuous = Measuring tape/Scale".
PBL Phase 3 | Independent/Group Investigation [30 minutes]
Hands-On Activity
(Student-led)

Data Collection Mission (30 min):

  • In groups of 3, students design a 3-question survey for the cafeteria problem. Must include 1 Qualitative and 1 Discrete Quantitative question.
  • Joyful Learning: Students move around the classroom collecting raw data from exactly 15 classmates using clipboards.
  • Collaboration: One asks, one tallies, one records.
PBL Phase 4 & 5 | Evaluate the Process [15 minutes]
Individual Task
(Teacher-monitored)

Review & 🎟️ Exit Ticket (15 min):

  • Bring class back. "Was collecting data messy? Next meeting, we organize it!"
  • Exit Ticket: Classify the following data type: "The weight of a watermelon" (Answer: Quantitative, Continuous).

📅 Meeting 2: Organising Data - Frequency & Stem-and-Leaf (Subtopic 4.2) [PBL Approach]

PBL Phase 1 & 2 | The Messy Data Problem [15 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Pair Work

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Show a massive, messy spreadsheet of unorganized numbers. "We have the data, but it's useless right now. How do we make it readable?"

Organizing for Study (10 min):

  • Review Tally Marks and Frequency Tables. Explain Grouped Frequency Tables for continuous data (e.g., $150 \le h < 160$).
PBL Phase 3 | Developing Artifacts (Stem-and-Leaf) [30 minutes]
Direct Instruction &
Hands-On

Direct Instruction — Slides 2-3 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Introduce Stem-and-Leaf diagrams. Explain that it organizes data *without losing the original numbers*.
  • Slide 3: Mindful Check: "Always include a Key! What does $4 | 2$ mean? Is it 42 or 4.2?"

Physical Sorting Activity (15 min):

  • Distribute envelopes with 20 two-digit number cards.
  • Groups physically arrange the cards on their desk into a stem-and-leaf shape.
Scaffold students: Low achievers only work with single-digit stems (10s, 20s). Extension students use decimal values for stems.
PBL Phase 4 & 5 | Presenting & Evaluating Solutions [35 minutes]
Independent Practice

Worksheet Task (25 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 4.2: Converting their raw cafeteria data from Meeting 1 into a neat Tally & Frequency Table.
  • Self-Regulated: Check that the total frequency adds up to the total number of people asked.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Draw a quick Stem-and-Leaf diagram for these test scores: $12, 15, 22, 25, 28, 31$.

📅 Meeting 3: Intro to PjBL - Bar Charts & Pictograms (Subtopic 4.3)

PjBL Phase 1 & 2 | Essential Question & Design Plan [20 minutes]
Teacher-led Intro &
Group Brainstorm

🪝 Hook & Essential Question (10 min):

  • Show a news clipping with a highly misleading bar chart (e.g., y-axis starts at 50 to exaggerate a tiny difference).
  • "How can we visually communicate data accurately and honestly? Welcome to your project: The Semesta Data Dashboard!"

Design Plan (10 min):

  • Groups of 3 choose a research topic (e.g., Screentime hours, Sleep hours, Transport to school).
  • Plan out what charts they will need to display their data.
PjBL Phase 3 | Skill Building (Bar Charts & Pictograms) [25 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 1-3 (20 min):

  • Slide 1: Rules of a Bar Chart: Equal gaps between bars, equal bar width, labeled axes, uniform scale.
  • Slide 2: Composite/Multiple Bar charts for comparing two groups (e.g., Boys vs Girls).
  • Slide 3: Pictograms. Must have a key! (e.g., 1 smiley face = 4 students. How to draw 2 students? Half a face).
Scaffold students: SEN students are provided with graph paper that has pre-drawn, scaled y-axes.
PjBL Phase 4 | Monitor & Create [35 minutes]
Project Work

Drafting Project Artifacts (25 min):

  • Groups use sample data (or their M1 data) to draft one precise Bar Chart and one Pictogram on A3 paper using rulers and markers.
  • Critical Thinking: Choosing an appropriate scale for the y-axis so the graph isn't too tiny or too huge.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • In a pictogram, if one Car icon represents 10 cars, how many icons do you draw to represent 25 cars? (Answer: 2.5 icons).

📅 Meeting 4: Pie Charts & Project Execution (Subtopic 4.3)

PjBL Phase 3 (Continued) | Skill Building (Pie Charts) [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Guided Practice

🪝 Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Show an image of a pizza sliced completely unequally. "If 4 people share this, is it fair? Pie charts represent fractions of a whole 360° circle."

Direct Instruction — Slides 2-3 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Formula: $\text{Angle} = \left(\frac{\text{Frequency}}{\text{Total Frequency}}\right) \times 360^\circ$.
  • Slide 3: Teacher demonstrates under a document camera how to use a protractor to draw the first angle, then rotate the protractor for the next slice.
  • Mindful Check: "Always check that your calculated angles add up to 360° before you start drawing!"
PjBL Phase 4 | Monitor Students & Project Progress [40 minutes]
Collaborative Project Work

Creating the Final Dashboard (40 min):

  • Groups finalize their "Semesta Data Dashboard" poster.
  • Must include: 1 Frequency Table, 1 Bar Chart/Pictogram, and 1 Pie Chart.
  • Teacher circulates, checking protractor usage and scale accuracy.
  • Collaboration: Divide tasks (one calculates angles, one draws the circle, one writes labels).
Scaffold students: Extension students must compare two pie charts of different sample sizes, explaining why the radius might be drawn differently to represent total volume.
PjBL Phase 4 | Formative Check [15 minutes]
Individual Task

🎟️ Exit Ticket (15 min):

  • A survey of 40 people showed 10 like apples. Calculate the angle for the "Apple" sector in a pie chart.

📅 Meeting 5: Project Presentation & Chapter Evaluation

PjBL Phase 5 | Assess the Outcome (Presentation) [25 minutes]
Student-led Gallery Walk

🪝 Gallery Walk (25 min):

  • Posters are displayed around the room.
  • Students walk around and evaluate peers' graphs based on 3 criteria: Title/Labels present? Scale accurate? Data correctly represented?
  • Teacher assesses communication and presentation artifacts.
PjBL Phase 5 | Evaluate the Experience (Summative) [55 minutes]
Summative Test
(Teacher-monitored)

Chapter 4 Test (45 min):

  • Remove visual aids. Distribute the Chapter 4 Evaluation.
  • Self-Regulated: Students pace themselves under exam conditions.

Closure & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Self-Reflection: "What was the hardest part of creating your Data Dashboard: Calculating, Drawing, or Organizing? How did you overcome it?"
🌟 Differentiation Strategies
Profile Strategies & Actions in Class
Extension (High Achievers) Introduce dual/composite bar charts and back-to-back stem-and-leaf diagrams for comparing two datasets. Ask them to explain why pie charts are limited when comparing total population sizes.
Scaffolding (Middle/Low) Provide pre-drawn axes for bar charts. Give protractors with clearly marked inner/outer circles and a step-by-step checklist for finding Pie Chart angles ($ \text{Fraction} \times 360 $).
EAL (English Additional Language) Provide a visual glossary: Discrete (Diskrit - bisa dihitung), Continuous (Kontinu - bisa diukur), Frequency (Frekuensi), Stem (Batang), Leaf (Daun).
SEN (Special Educational Needs) Use physical objects (blocks, stickers) to construct pictograms before drawing them. Reduce the dataset size (e.g., $n=10$ instead of $n=40$) to minimize arithmetic fatigue during angle calculations.

📝 Worksheets Compilation (For Practice & Homework)

Worksheet 4.1 & 4.2: Collecting & Organising Data

1. Classify the following data as Qualitative, Quantitative Discrete, or Quantitative Continuous:
a) The height of students in Grade 9.
b) The number of cars in a parking lot.
c) The color of backpacks in the classroom.

2. Organize the following test scores into a grouped frequency table using classes 10-19, 20-29, 30-39:
$15, 22, 18, 35, 29, 21, 14, 33, 27, 20, 19, 38$

3. Create a Stem-and-Leaf diagram for the data in question 2. Remember to include a Key!

Worksheet 4.3: Drawing Charts

1. A survey of 30 students asked about their transport to school: Bus (12), Car (8), Walk (6), Bicycle (4).
a) Draw a Bar Chart for this data. Ensure axes are labeled correctly.
b) Draw a Pictogram for this data where 1 icon represents 2 students.

2. Calculate the angles needed to draw a pie chart for the transport data above. Show your calculations for each category.

💯 Chapter 4 Evaluation (Used in Meeting 5)

Instructions: Answer all questions. Show all working steps clearly. Total: 30 Marks.

Part A: Data Classification & Tables (10 Marks)

1. Which of the following is an example of continuous data? (1 mark)
A) Number of siblings    B) Eye color    C) Time taken to run 100m    D) Shoe size

2. A teacher records the marks of 15 students out of 50:
$42, 35, 48, 29, 31, 45, 33, 40, 36, 41, 28, 39, 44, 37, 49$
Draw an ordered Stem-and-Leaf diagram for these marks. You MUST include a Key. (4 marks)

3. Based on your Stem-and-Leaf diagram, how many students scored 40 or above? (2 marks)

4. Give one advantage of using a Stem-and-Leaf diagram instead of a grouped frequency table. (3 marks)

Part B: Chart Construction & Interpretation (20 Marks)

5. The table below shows the favorite sports of 60 students.

SportFootballBasketballBadmintonSwimming
Frequency25151010

a) Calculate the sector angle for "Football" in a pie chart. Show your working. (3 marks)

b) Calculate the sector angle for "Basketball". (2 marks)

c) Draw an accurate Pie Chart to display this data. Label each sector clearly. (5 marks)

6. Look at the data from question 5.
a) Draw a Bar Chart representing this data. Pay attention to your axis scale and gaps between bars. (6 marks)
b) A student wants to draw a pictogram for this data using a "Ball" symbol. If one "Ball" represents 5 students, how many symbols are drawn for Badminton? (2 marks)

7. Interpretation: Looking at your completed charts, write one conclusive sentence about the students' sport preferences that a school principal could use to make a decision about buying equipment. (2 marks)
