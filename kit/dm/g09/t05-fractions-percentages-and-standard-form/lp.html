Lesson Plan: Fractions, Percentages & Standard Form

School: SMP Semesta 2026/2025 | Grade: 9

Time Allocation: 5 Meetings (2 x 40 minutes = 80 mins per meeting)

📌 Topic: 5. Fractions, percentages and standard form

Reference: Cambridge IGCSE® Math Core & Extended

Method: Discovery Learning

Approach: Deep Learning, Hands-on, Culturally STEM

Character Focus: Critical Thinking, Creativity, Self-Regulated, Collaboration

Differentiation: Extension, Scaffolding, EAL, SEN

🎯 Objectives

  • C1.4, E1.4: Use the language and notation of proper/improper fractions and mixed numbers; Recognise equivalence and convert between forms.
  • C1.6, E1.6: Use the four operations for calculations with fractions.
  • C1.13, E1.13: Calculate a given percentage of a quantity; Express one quantity as a percentage of another; Calculate percentage increase/decrease.
  • C1.8, E1.8: Use standard form $A \times 10^n$; Convert in and out of standard form; Calculate with values in standard form.

📅 Meeting 1: Revisiting & Adding/Subtracting Fractions (Subtopics 5.1 & 5.2)

Phase 1 & 2 | Stimulation & Problem Statement [15 minutes]
Interactive Hook
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Display a traditional recipe for Klepon. "To make a large batch for a school festival, we need $1 \frac{1}{2}$ cups of glutinous rice flour. We only have $\frac{3}{4}$ of a cup left in the pantry."
  • Meaningful: Ask: "How much MORE flour do we need to buy? Can we easily subtract $\frac{3}{4}$ from $1 \frac{1}{2}$?"

Problem Statement (5 min):

  • How do we mathematically compare, add, and subtract pieces of a whole when the pieces are cut into different sizes (denominators)?
Phase 3 | Data Collection (Hands-On) [20 minutes]
Hands-On Activity
(Student-led)

Fraction Circles Activity (20 min):

  • Distribute sets of plastic fraction circles (or printed cut-outs).
  • Instruct groups to physically build $1 \frac{1}{2}$ using 'Halves'. Then try to take away $\frac{3}{4}$.
  • Joyful Discovery: Students will physically realize they cannot take "quarters" out of "halves" until they swap the "halves" for "quarters" (finding equivalent fractions).
Phase 4 & 5 | Data Processing & Verification [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Vocabulary: Proper, Improper, Mixed Numbers. Convert $1 \frac{1}{2}$ to $\frac{3}{2}$.
  • Slide 3: Rule Verification: To add/subtract, we MUST have a Common Denominator. (Show the LCM method from Chapter 1).
  • Slide 4: Teacher models mathematically what they just did physically: $\frac{6}{4} - \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3}{4}$.
  • Mindful Check: "Do we add/subtract the denominators? Why not?" (The denominator is just the 'size' of the slice).
Scaffold students: EAL learners draw boxes for numerators and denominators. SEN students continue using physical fraction bars alongside written work.
Phase 6 | Generalization & Practice [25 minutes]
Independent Task
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (15 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 5.1 & 5.2A (Addition/Subtraction & Conversions).

🎟️ Generalization / Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Write the general rule for adding fractions. Calculate: $\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4}$.

📅 Meeting 2: Multiplying and Dividing Fractions (Subtopic 5.2)

Phase 1 & 2 | Stimulation & Problem Statement [15 minutes]
Visual Hook
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Show a map of a Sawah (rice paddy). "A farmer owns $\frac{3}{4}$ of a hectare of land. He wants to give exactly $\frac{1}{2}$ of his land to his son. How much of the total hectare does the son get?"
  • Critical Thinking: "Does 'of' mean add, subtract, multiply, or divide?"
Phase 3 & 4 | Data Collection & Processing (Paper Folding) [25 minutes]
Hands-On Activity
(Student-led)

Paper Folding Proof (25 min):

  • Give each student a blank A4 paper (represents 1 hectare).
  • Fold vertically to shade $\frac{3}{4}$ (the farmer's land).
  • Now fold horizontally in half to find $\frac{1}{2}$ OF the $\frac{3}{4}$.
  • Unfold. Count the double-shaded rectangles (3) out of the total rectangles formed (8). Answer: $\frac{3}{8}$.
Phase 5 | Verification (Formalizing Rules) [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Multiplication Rule: Top $\times$ Top, Bottom $\times$ Bottom. $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3}{8}$. (Verify this matches the paper folds!).
  • Slide 3: Division Rule: "Keep, Change, Flip" (Multiply by the Reciprocal). Explain *why* (dividing by half is the same as multiplying by 2).
  • Slide 4: Working with Mixed Numbers: MUST convert to improper fractions before multiplying/dividing!
Scaffold students: Extension students prove why 'Keep Change Flip' works algebraically.
Phase 6 | Generalization & Practice [20 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (10 min):

  • Worksheet 5.2B. Includes multiplication and division word problems.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Calculate in simplest form: $\frac{4}{5} \div \frac{2}{3}$.

📅 Meeting 3: Percentages (Subtopic 5.3)

Phase 1 & 2 | Stimulation & Problem Statement [15 minutes]
Real-World Scenario

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Show a photo of a Batik shop in Semarang. "A Batik shirt costs Rp 250,000. There is a 'Diskon 20%' sign. At the cashier, there is a 10% tax added. Is the final price exactly Rp 250,000?"
  • Students vote Yes or No. (Problem statement: Percentage changes are sequential, not additive!).
Phase 3 & 4 | Data Collection & Processing [25 minutes]
Collaborative
(Student-led)

Percentage Multiplier Discovery (25 min):

  • Groups calculate 20% of 250,000 and subtract it (Method 1).
  • Teacher asks: "Is there a faster way? If you lose 20%, what percentage do you KEEP?" (80%).
  • Students calculate $0.80 \times 250,000$. Compare answers. They discover the **Multiplier Method**.
  • Self-Regulated: Students realize calculating multipliers saves time.
Phase 5 | Verification [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Calculating Multipliers. (15% increase = $1.15$, 30% decrease = $0.70$).
  • Slide 3: Percentage Change Formula: $\frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{Original}} \times 100\%$.
  • Slide 4: Expressing one quantity as a percentage of another. (e.g., 40 out of 50 students).
Scaffold students: Provide low achievers a "Multiplier Translation Table" to help them map "Increase 5%" to "1.05".
Phase 6 | Generalization & Practice [25 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (15 min):

  • Worksheet 5.3 solving discount, tax, and population growth problems.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • A phone's battery drops from 80% to 60%. What is the percentage decrease? (Careful: it is $\frac{20}{80} \times 100 = 25\%$, not 20%!).

📅 Meeting 4: Standard Form (Subtopic 5.4)

Phase 1 & 2 | Stimulation & Problem Statement [15 minutes]
Visual Hook
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Culturally STEM: "The distance from Sabang to Merauke is roughly $5,245,000$ meters. A yeast cell in Tempeh is about $0.000004$ meters wide."
  • Ask: "Write both numbers on your whiteboard. If you accidentally miss one zero, what happens?" (Catastrophic error in science/engineering).
  • Problem: How do scientists write massive/microscopic numbers without writing zeros?
Phase 3 & 4 | Data Collection & Processing [20 minutes]
Collaborative
(Student-led)

Jumping Decimals (20 min):

  • Students use powers of 10 from Chapter 1.
  • Write $5 \times 10^3$. Calculate it manually ($5 \times 1000 = 5000$).
  • Write $4 \times 10^{-6}$. Calculate manually ($4 \div 1000000 = 0.000004$).
  • Mindful Check: Students discover that the exponent relates exactly to how many "jumps" the decimal point makes.
Phase 5 | Verification [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Formal Definition: $A \times 10^n$, where $1 \le A < 10$.
  • Slide 3: Why is $34 \times 10^5$ mathematically WRONG in standard form? (Because 34 is larger than 10. Fix it: $3.4 \times 10^6$).
  • Slide 4: Multiplying/Dividing standard form. Add/Subtract indices!
Scaffold students: EAL teach "Scientific Notation" as a synonym. SEN students draw physical "frog jumps" under the zeroes.
Phase 6 | Generalization & Practice [25 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (15 min):

  • Worksheet 5.4. Convert numbers into standard form and out as ordinary numbers.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Write $0.000782$ in standard form.

📅 Meeting 5: Chapter Review & Summative Evaluation

Phase 1-3 | Concept Review (Mini-Whiteboards) [20 minutes]
Whole Class Activity

🪝 Rapid Fire Review (20 min):

  • Teacher flashes questions on the screen (Fractions, Multipliers, Standard Form).
  • Students have 30 seconds to write the answer and hold up whiteboards.
  • Teacher targets misconceptions immediately before the exam.
Phase 4-6 | Summative Evaluation [60 minutes]
Summative Test
(Teacher-monitored)

Chapter 5 Test (50 min):

  • Distribute the Chapter 5 Evaluation.
  • Self-Regulated: Students pace themselves under strict exam conditions.

Closure & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Self-Reflection: "How has understanding Standard Form changed your view of very large numbers in science?"
🌟 Differentiation Strategies
Profile Strategies & Actions in Class
Extension (High Achievers) Challenge with compound percentage problems (e.g., compound interest, or repeated percentage depreciation). Provide standard form addition/subtraction problems (which require making indices match first).
Scaffolding (Middle/Low) Provide fraction bars/circles to verify arithmetic. Give a "Percentage Multiplier Translation Sheet" (e.g., 20% discount $\rightarrow$ Multiply by 0.8).
EAL (English Additional Language) Provide a bilingual glossary: Numerator (Pembilang), Denominator (Penyebut), Proper Fraction (Pecahan Biasa), Mixed Number (Pecahan Campuran). Emphasize visual steps over word problems.
SEN (Special Educational Needs) Draw visual "loops" or "frog jumps" physically on paper when converting decimals to Standard Form. Highlight the target integer ($A$) in a bright color.

📝 Worksheets Compilation (For Practice & Homework)

Worksheet 5.1 & 5.2: Fractions

1. Convert $3 \frac{2}{5}$ into an improper fraction.

2. Calculate the following in their simplest form:
a) $\frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{6}$
b) $2 \frac{1}{2} - 1 \frac{2}{3}$
c) $\frac{5}{8} \times \frac{4}{15}$
d) $1 \frac{3}{4} \div \frac{7}{8}$

3. A recipe requires $\frac{2}{3}$ cup of sugar. How much sugar is needed for $2 \frac{1}{2}$ batches of the recipe?

Worksheet 5.3: Percentages

1. Express 45 as a percentage of 60.

2. A jacket costs Rp 300,000. It is on sale with a 15% discount. Calculate the sale price.

3. The population of a town increased from 40,000 to 46,000. Calculate the percentage increase.

Worksheet 5.4: Standard Form

1. Write the following numbers in standard form ($A \times 10^n$):
a) $45,000,000$
b) $0.0000072$

2. Write the following as ordinary numbers:
a) $3.1 \times 10^4$
b) $8.5 \times 10^{-3}$

3. Calculate $(2 \times 10^3) \times (4 \times 10^5)$ and leave your answer in standard form.

💯 Chapter 5 Evaluation (Used in Meeting 5)

Instructions: Answer all questions. Show all working steps clearly. Fractions must be fully simplified. Total: 35 Marks.

Part A: Fractions (12 Marks)

1. Arrange the following fractions in ascending order (smallest to largest): $\frac{2}{3}, \frac{5}{8}, \frac{3}{4}$. Show your working (e.g., using a common denominator). (3 marks)

2. Calculate and write the answer as a mixed number: $3 \frac{1}{4} + 1 \frac{5}{6}$. (4 marks)

3. A carpenter has a plank of wood $4 \frac{1}{2}$ meters long. He cuts it into pieces that are each $\frac{3}{4}$ meter long. How many pieces can he cut? (5 marks)

Part B: Percentages (11 Marks)

4. Calculate 35% of 240. (2 marks)

5. A bicycle initially costs Rp 1,500,000. The price is increased by 12%.
a) What is the multiplier for a 12% increase? (1 mark)
b) Calculate the new price of the bicycle. (3 marks)

6. During a sale, a laptop's price drops from Rp 8,000,000 to Rp 6,400,000. Calculate the percentage discount. (5 marks)

Part C: Standard Form (12 Marks)

7. Write $156,000$ in standard form. (2 marks)

8. Write $4.02 \times 10^{-4}$ as an ordinary decimal number. (2 marks)

9. The mass of the Earth is approximately $5.97 \times 10^{24}$ kg. The mass of Mars is approximately $6.4 \times 10^{23}$ kg.
a) Which planet is heavier? Give a reason. (2 marks)
b) Calculate the combined mass of Earth and Mars. Give your answer in standard form. (6 marks)
