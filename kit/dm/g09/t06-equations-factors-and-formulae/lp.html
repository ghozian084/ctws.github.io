Lesson Plan: Equations, Factors and Formulae

School: SMP Semesta 2026/2025 | Grade: 9

Time Allocation: 5 Meetings (2 x 40 minutes = 80 mins per meeting)

📌 Topic: 6. Equations, factors and formulae

Reference: Cambridge IGCSE® Math Core & Extended

Method: 5E & Mathematics Modelling

Approach: Deep Learning & Culturally STEM

Character Focus: Critical Thinking, Creativity, Self-Regulated, Collaboration

Differentiation: Extension, Scaffolding, EAL, SEN

🎯 Objectives

  • C2.5, E2.5: Solve linear equations in one unknown.
  • C2.2, E2.2: Factorise by extracting common factors.
  • C2.5, E2.5: Change the subject of simple formulas.

📅 Meeting 1: Solving Linear Equations (Subtopic 6.1)

E1 ENGAGE | Hook & Physical Modelling [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Display a picture of a traditional Indonesian market scale (Timbangan Pasar).
  • Ask: "If the scale is perfectly balanced, what happens if I take 1 kg off the left side? How do I make it balance again?"

Math Modeling Step 1 & 2: Formulate (10 min):

  • Meaningful: Let a mysterious box of apples be $x$. If 2 boxes plus 1 kg equals 7 kg, we write $2x + 1 = 7$.
  • Teacher draws the balance scale on the board mapping to the equation.
E2 EXPLORE | Balance Method [20 minutes]
Collaborative Group Work
(Student-led)

Math Modeling Step 3: Solve (20 min):

  • Distribute Algebra Tiles or physical counter tokens.
  • Groups physically build the equation $3x + 2 = 11$.
  • Instruct them to maintain the balance. "Take away 2 from BOTH sides." "Now divide the remaining equally among the 3 $x$ boxes."
  • Joyful Learning: Groups race to solve physical equations.
E3 EXPLAIN | Formalizing the Algebra [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Translating the physical balance to written algebra using Inverse Operations (SAMDOB - reverse BODMAS).
  • Slide 3: Example: $4x - 5 = 15 \rightarrow 4x = 20 \rightarrow x = 5$.
  • Slide 4: Equations with unknowns on BOTH sides: $5x + 2 = 3x + 10$. Strategy: "Move the smallest $x$ first!"
Scaffold students: SEN students draw a vertical line down the '=' sign to separate LHS and RHS. Require them to write the inverse operation (e.g., "-2") on both sides.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [25 minutes]
Independent Practice
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (15 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 6.1 (Part 1).
  • Mindful Check: "Math Modeling Step 4: Validate. Substitute your answer back into the original equation to check if LHS = RHS!"

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Solve for $x$: $6x - 4 = 20$. Show all steps.

📅 Meeting 2: Complex Equations (Brackets and Fractions) (Subtopic 6.1)

E1 & E2 | Hook & Math Modeling Group Task [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Small Group Work

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Culturally STEM: "You are booking a cultural trip to Borobudur for 3 families. Each family has $x$ adults and 2 children. If the total number of people going is 15, how many adults per family?"

Math Modeling Step 1 & 2 (20 min):

  • Groups formulate the equation: $3(x + 2) = 15$.
  • Critical Thinking: Groups must find TWO different mathematical ways to solve it (Method 1: Expand brackets first. Method 2: Divide by 3 first).
E3 EXPLAIN | Equations with Brackets and Fractions [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Review expanding brackets. Model solving $2(3x - 1) = 4x + 8$.
  • Slide 3: Equations with fractional coefficients: $\frac{x}{4} + 3 = 8$. Rule: Multiply to destroy the fraction!
  • Slide 4: Equations where the whole expression is a fraction: $\frac{2x - 5}{3} = 5$.
Scaffold students: Extension students solve equations where the unknown is in the denominator (e.g., $\frac{12}{x} = 4$).
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (25 min):

  • Worksheet 6.1 (Part 2). Students solve multi-step equations involving brackets and fractions.
  • Self-Regulated: Students verify their own answers using substitution before handing in the worksheet.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Solve for $y$: $5(y - 2) = 3y + 12$.

📅 Meeting 3: Factorising Algebraic Expressions (Subtopic 6.2)

E1 ENGAGE | Contextual Hook [15 minutes]
Visual Q&A
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (15 min):

  • Show an image of traditional Tegel Kunci (Javanese floor tiles).
  • "We have a rectangular tiled floor with an area of $3x + 12$. If the width is a whole number, what is the maximum width it could be?"
  • Meaningful: Lead students to realize they need to find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of 3 and 12. This is reverse expansion!
E2 EXPLORE | The Reverse Area Model [20 minutes]
Pair Work
(Student-led)

Area Model Backwards (20 min):

  • Draw a rectangle split into two. Inside the boxes, write $4x$ and $20$.
  • Ask pairs: "What number goes on the outside (the width) that fits perfectly into BOTH $4x$ and $20$?"
  • Pairs try different combinations (e.g., pulling out a 2 vs pulling out a 4). They discover that pulling out the HCF is the most efficient (fully factorised).
E3 EXPLAIN | Extracting Common Factors [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Define "Factorising" as putting the expression back into brackets. It is the exact opposite of Expanding.
  • Slide 3: Step 1: Find HCF of the numbers. Step 2: Find HCF of the letters. Step 3: Open bracket and divide.
  • Slide 4: Model factorising $6x^2 + 9x \rightarrow 3x(2x + 3)$.
Scaffold students: Low achievers explicitly write out the factors of each term underneath the expression before deciding on the HCF.
E4 & E5 | Independent Practice & Exit [30 minutes]
Independent Task
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (20 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 6.2 on single-bracket factorisation.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Factorise fully: $15y^2 - 10y$.

📅 Meeting 4: Rearrangement of a Formula (Subtopic 6.3)

E1 & E2 | Hook & Conceptual Shift [20 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Class Debate

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • "To make a traditional Rendang, a chef uses an oven set in Fahrenheit. The formula is $F = \frac{9}{5}C + 32$."
  • "What if we know the Fahrenheit temperature and want to find Celsius? Do we have to guess and check every time? No, we change the subject of the formula!"

Explore (10 min):

  • Write $A = L \times W$. Ask: "How do we get $L$ by itself?" (Divide both sides by $W \rightarrow L = \frac{A}{W}$).
E3 EXPLAIN | Changing the Subject [25 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (25 min):

  • Slide 2: Define "Subject of a formula" (the variable sitting alone on one side of the equals sign).
  • Slide 3: Rule: Changing the subject uses the EXACT SAME inverse operations as solving equations, but the answer will be an algebraic expression, not a number.
  • Slide 4: Model changing subject: $y = 3x - 4$. Make $x$ the subject. $\rightarrow x = \frac{y + 4}{3}$.
Scaffold students: EAL students highlight the "Target Subject" variable in bright yellow so they remember which letter needs to be isolated.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min):

  • Worksheet 6.3. Students rearrange common STEM formulas (e.g., $v = u + at$ make $t$ the subject; $P = 2L + 2W$ make $W$ the subject).
  • Self-Regulated: Students must verify by substituting arbitrary numbers into both the original and rearranged formulas.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Make $m$ the subject of the formula: $T = 4m + k$.

📅 Meeting 5: Complex Rearrangement & Chapter Evaluation (Subtopic 6.3)

E1 & E2 | Advanced Rearrangement (Roots and Powers) [20 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Guided Practice

🪝 Hook & Explore — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Culturally STEM: Show the formula for the area of a circular gong: $A = \pi r^2$. "How do we find the radius if we only know the Area?"
  • Ask students what the inverse of a square is. (Square root).

Explain (10 min):

  • Model rearranging formulas involving powers and roots: $r = \sqrt{\frac{A}{\pi}}$.
  • Mindful: Emphasize that the square root covers the ENTIRE fraction.
E4 & E5 | Chapter Evaluation & Closure [60 minutes]
Summative Test
(Teacher-monitored)

Chapter 6 Test (50 min):

  • Distribute the Chapter 6 Evaluation covering Solving Equations, Factorising, and Rearranging Formulas.
  • Self-Regulated: Students pace themselves under strict exam conditions.

Closure & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Self-Reflection: "Which is more difficult for you: Solving an equation for a number, or rearranging a formula for a letter? Why?"
🌟 Differentiation Strategies
Profile Strategies & Actions in Class
Extension (High Achievers) Challenge with equations where the unknown is in the denominator. Provide rearrangement formulas where the subject appears *twice* (requiring factorisation to isolate it, e.g., $y = \frac{x+1}{x-2}$ make $x$ the subject).
Scaffolding (Middle/Low) Provide physical Algebra Tiles for equation solving. Give a visual flow-chart mapping "SAMDOB" (reverse BODMAS) to help them decide which operation to undo first when rearranging formulas.
EAL (English Additional Language) Provide a bilingual glossary: Subject (Subjek/Fokus), Formula (Rumus), Factorise (Faktorkan), Equation (Persamaan). Ensure they understand "Change the subject to x" means "Make it x = ...".
SEN (Special Educational Needs) Use high-contrast printouts. Require drawing a vertical line down the '=' sign to separate LHS and RHS to maintain visual structure. Highlight the target variable in a bright color.

📝 Worksheets Compilation (For Practice & Homework)

Worksheet 6.1: Solving Linear Equations

1. Solve the following equations:
a) $4x + 7 = 23$
b) $\frac{y}{3} - 4 = 6$
c) $5m - 3 = 2m + 12$

2. Math Modeling: A rectangular garden has length $(2x + 5)$ and width $x$. The perimeter is 40 meters.
a) Formulate an equation for the perimeter.
b) Solve the equation to find $x$.

3. Solve for $p$: $3(p - 4) = 2(p + 5)$.

Worksheet 6.2: Factorising Expressions

1. Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) for the following pairs of terms:
a) $12x$ and $18$    b) $5y^2$ and $15y$

2. Factorise the following expressions completely:
a) $8a + 12$
b) $14m - 21n$
c) $6x^2 + 10x$
d) $15x^2y - 20xy^2$

Worksheet 6.3: Rearrangement of a Formula

1. Make $x$ the subject of the formula: $y = 4x + 5$.

2. The formula for the perimeter of a rectangle is $P = 2L + 2W$. Rearrange the formula to make $W$ the subject.

3. Make $v$ the subject of the kinematic formula: $E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.

💯 Chapter 6 Evaluation (Used in Meeting 5)

Instructions: Answer all questions. Show all working steps clearly. Total: 30 Marks.

Part A: Solving Equations (12 Marks)

1. Solve for $x$: $7x - 9 = 26$. (2 marks)

2. Solve for $y$: $\frac{3y + 4}{2} = 11$. (3 marks)

3. Solve for $m$: $6m - 5 = 4m + 9$. (3 marks)

4. Solve for $k$: $4(2k - 1) = 3(k + 7)$. (4 marks)

Part B: Factorising (8 Marks)

5. Factorise completely:
a) $18x + 24$ (2 marks)
b) $9y^2 - 12y$ (3 marks)
c) $14a^2b + 21ab^2$ (3 marks)

Part C: Formulae & Modeling (10 Marks)

6. Make $h$ the subject of the formula: $A = \frac{b \times h}{2}$. (2 marks)

7. The formula connecting velocity ($v$), initial velocity ($u$), acceleration ($a$), and time ($t$) is $v = u + at$. Rearrange to make $a$ the subject. (3 marks)

8. STEM Context Problem: The volume of a cylinder (like a traditional Indonesian drum) is given by $V = \pi r^2 h$.
a) Make $r$ the subject of the formula. (3 marks)
b) If the Volume is $314 \text{ cm}^3$ and the height ($h$) is $4 \text{ cm}$, calculate the radius $r$. (Use $\pi \approx 3.14$). (2 marks)
