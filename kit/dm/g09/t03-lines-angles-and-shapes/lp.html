Lesson Plan: Lines, Angles and Shapes

School: SMP Semesta 2026/2025 | Grade: 9

Time Allocation: 6 Meetings (2 x 40 minutes = 80 mins per meeting)

📌 Topic: 3. Lines, angles and shapes

Reference: Cambridge IGCSE® Math Core & Extended

Method: 5E, Hands-on Activity

Approach: Deep Learning & Culturally STEM

Character Focus: Critical Thinking, Creativity, Self-Regulated, Collaboration

Differentiation: Extension, Scaffolding, EAL, SEN

🎯 Objectives

  • C4.6, E4.6: Calculate unknown angles using geometrical properties. Angle sum of triangle = 180°. Angle sum of quadrilateral = 360°. Know and use properties of regular polygons.
  • C4.1, E4.1: Use and interpret geometrical terms like line, parallel, perpendicular. Interpret vocabulary of triangles, special quadrilaterals, polygons, and circles.
  • C4.7, E4.7: Calculate unknown angles using circle theorems.
  • C4.2, E4.2: Measure and draw lines and angles; Construct a triangle given the lengths of all sides.

📅 Meeting 1: Lines, Angles, and Transversals (Subtopic 3.1)

E1 ENGAGE | Contextual Hook [10 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Display a piece of traditional Indonesian *Kain Tenun Lurik*. Ask: "What geometric patterns do you notice here?" (Straight lines running parallel and perpendicular).
  • Meaningful: Explain that weavers use a loom to keep threads perfectly parallel. What happens if a thread crosses them diagonally? (A transversal is formed).
E2 EXPLORE | Hands-On Activity [25 minutes]
Collaborative
(Student-led)

Washi Tape Desks (25 min):

  • Distribute washi/masking tape and protractors to groups of 3.
  • Instruct students to tape two parallel lines across their desk, and one transversal line cutting through them.
  • Students must measure all 8 angles created.
  • Joyful & Mindful: Ask groups to color-code angles that are equal. They will discover the relationships visually.
E3 EXPLAIN | Formalizing Geometric Rules [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–5 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Introduce vocabulary: Parallel, Perpendicular, Transversal.
  • Slide 3: Formalize the patterns from the tape activity: Corresponding Angles (F shape).
  • Slide 4: Alternate Interior Angles (Z shape).
  • Slide 5: Co-interior Angles (C shape) summing to 180°.
Scaffold students: Use tracing paper for SEN/Low students. Have them trace an angle and slide it down the transversal to prove Corresponding angles match.
E4 & E5 | Practice & Evaluation [30 minutes]
Independent Task
& Exit Slip

Elaborate (20 min):

  • Students complete Worksheet 3.1 solving unknown angles involving parallel lines.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Draw two parallel lines and a transversal. If one acute angle is 40°, find the alternate interior angle and state its value.

📅 Meeting 2: The Magic of Triangles (Subtopic 3.2)

E1 ENGAGE | Architecture Hook [10 minutes]
Interactive Q&A
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Show images of *Rumah Gadang* (Minangkabau) roofs. "Why are roofs built as triangles? Why not squares?" (Triangles are rigid and strong).
  • Discuss structural stability. Introduce triangle classification by sides (Equilateral, Isosceles, Scalene).
E2 EXPLORE | Hands-On Proof [20 minutes]
Hands-On Activity
(Student-led)

Paper Tearing Proof (20 min):

  • Distribute colored paper and scissors. Students cut out a random large triangle (scalene).
  • Instruct them to tear off all three corners (vertices).
  • Critical Thinking: Ask: "Can you arrange these three corners to form a straight line?"
  • Students tape the corners onto a straight line in their notebooks, physically proving that the interior angles sum to 180°.
E3 EXPLAIN | Angles and Vocabulary [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Formalize the formula: Sum of interior angles = 180°.
  • Slide 3: Isosceles properties (base angles are equal) & Equilateral properties (all angles 60°).
  • Slide 4: Exterior angle theorem: Exterior angle = sum of two opposite interior angles.
Scaffold students: EAL students map visual symbols (tick marks on sides, arcs on angles) to the English vocabulary words.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min):

  • Worksheet 3.2. Includes finding missing angles in isosceles triangles combined with parallel lines.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • In an isosceles triangle, the top angle is 50°. What are the sizes of the two base angles?

📅 Meeting 3: Special Quadrilaterals (Subtopic 3.3)

E1 ENGAGE | Cultural Craft [10 minutes]
Class Discussion
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Show a traditional Indonesian *Layang-layang* (Kite). "How do we ensure the kite flies perfectly straight?"
  • Discuss symmetry. Introduce the mathematical definition of a Kite (two pairs of adjacent equal sides).
E2 EXPLORE | Straw Construction [20 minutes]
Group Work
(Student-led)

Building Quadrilaterals (20 min):

  • Distribute plastic straws (cut to various lengths) and string/pipe cleaners.
  • Ask groups to build: 1) A square, 2) A rectangle.
  • Joyful & Mindful: Ask them to "squash" their square. What shape does it become? (Rhombus). Squash the rectangle? (Parallelogram).
E3 EXPLAIN | Quadrilateral Hierarchy [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–5 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Properties of Parallelograms (opposite sides parallel/equal, opposite angles equal, diagonals bisect).
  • Slide 3: Properties of Rhombuses, Trapeziums, and Kites.
  • Slide 4: Why do the interior angles of ANY quadrilateral sum to 360°? (Show drawing a diagonal splits it into two 180° triangles).
Scaffold students: Provide a 'Properties Checklist' matrix where students tick off features (e.g., Diagonals cross at 90°) for each shape.
E4 & E5 | Practice & Evaluation [30 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (20 min):

  • Worksheet 3.3. Students solve for missing angles using properties of parallelograms and kites.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Name a quadrilateral that has diagonals intersecting at 90°, but is NOT a square. (Rhombus or Kite).

📅 Meeting 4: Polygons (Subtopic 3.4)

E1 ENGAGE | Real-World Geometry [10 minutes]
Visual Hook
(Teacher-led)

🪝 Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Show images of hexagonal paving blocks from Ungaran square, and honeycomb patterns.
  • Ask: "Why do bees and engineers love hexagons? Why not pentagons?" (Hexagons tessellate perfectly because their angles fit around a point).
E2 EXPLORE | Deriving the Formula [20 minutes]
Collaborative
(Student-led)

Triangle Splitting (20 min):

  • Give groups drawings of a Pentagon, Hexagon, and Heptagon.
  • Instruct them to pick ONE vertex and draw diagonals to split the shape into triangles.
  • Critical Thinking: Fill in a table: Sides vs Triangles formed. Deduce the pattern: Number of triangles = $n - 2$.
E3 EXPLAIN | Interior & Exterior Angles [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Formalize the Interior Angle Sum formula: $(n - 2) \times 180^\circ$.
  • Slide 3: Define "Regular Polygon" (all sides/angles equal). How to find ONE interior angle.
  • Slide 4: Exterior Angles. Show the "walking around the polygon" animation to prove Exterior Angles ALWAYS sum to 360°.
Scaffold students: Extension students reverse engineer the formula (e.g., "A regular polygon has an interior angle of 144°. How many sides?").
E4 & E5 | Application & Exit [30 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (20 min):

  • Worksheet 3.4 computing interior/exterior angles of various regular polygons.

🎟️ Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • What is the size of one exterior angle of a regular octagon (8 sides)?

📅 Meeting 5: Circles & Basic Circle Theorems (Subtopic 3.5)

E1 & E2 | Hook & Thales' Discovery [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Hands-On

🪝 Hook (5 min):

  • Show an image of a Gamelan Gong. Discuss the anatomy (center point, outer rim). Introduce vocabulary: Radius, Diameter, Chord, Arc, Sector, Tangent.

Explore — Thales's Theorem (20 min):

  • Distribute circular cut-outs. Students fold them exactly in half to find the diameter crease.
  • Joyful Discovery: Pick any point on the curve, draw lines to the ends of the diameter. Measure the angle. It will ALWAYS be 90°!
E3 EXPLAIN | Introductory Circle Theorems [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 1-4 (20 min):

  • Slide 1: Recap vocabulary and Angle in a Semicircle = 90°.
  • Slide 2: Angle at the center is TWICE the angle at the circumference (subtended by the same arc).
  • Slide 3: Angles subtended by the same arc at the circumference are equal ("Bowtie" shape).
  • Slide 4: Cyclic Quadrilaterals (opposite angles sum to 180°).
Scaffold students: Low achievers focus only on the Semicircle=90° and Bowtie rules. Give color-coded diagrams where arcs are highlighted.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Worksheet 3.5. Finding missing angles using the three basic circle theorems.
  • Exit Ticket: Draw a cyclic quadrilateral. If one angle is 100°, what is the angle directly opposite to it?

📅 Meeting 6: Constructions & Chapter Evaluation (Subtopic 3.6)

E1 & E2 | Engineering Blueprints [20 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Hands-On Tool Use

🪝 Hook & Explore (20 min):

  • "Before CAD software, architects used a compass and straightedge to ensure perfect proportions."
  • Teacher demonstrates how to construct a triangle given 3 side lengths (SSS) using a compass.
  • Students practice constructing a triangle with sides 5cm, 6cm, and 8cm in their books.
  • Mindful: Emphasize NOT erasing construction arcs—they are proof of the method!
E4 & E5 | Summative Chapter Evaluation [60 minutes]
Summative Test
(Teacher-monitored)

Chapter 3 Test (50 min):

  • Distribute the Chapter 3 Evaluation covering lines, angles, polygons, and circle theorems.
  • Self-Regulated: Students manage their time across the sections.

Closure & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Self-Reflection: "Which topic (Lines, Polygons, or Circles) did you find most challenging? How will you review it?"
🌟 Differentiation Strategies
Profile Strategies & Actions in Class
Extension (High Achievers) Introduce algebraic expressions into geometry problems (e.g., angles in a triangle given as $2x$, $x+10$, and $3x-40$. Solve for $x$). Provide complex multi-step circle theorem puzzles requiring justification for each step.
Scaffolding (Middle/Low) Provide tracing paper for angle translation on parallel lines. Supply a visual "cheat sheet" containing all polygon formulas and circle theorem diagrams. Guide step-by-step for construction tasks.
EAL (English Additional Language) Provide a highly visual, bilingual glossary: Parallel (Sejajar), Perpendicular (Tegak lurus), Isosceles (Sama kaki), Equilateral (Sama sisi), Chord (Tali busur). Emphasize matching the English word to the visual symbol (like arrows for parallel).
SEN (Special Educational Needs) Use physical manipulatives continuously (washi tape, paper tearing, plastic straws) to ground abstract geometrical concepts in tactile reality. Use large, uncluttered diagrams on worksheets.

📝 Worksheets Compilation (For Practice & Homework)

Worksheet 3.1 & 3.2: Lines and Triangles

1. Two parallel lines are intersected by a transversal. If one angle is 110°, find the value of its corresponding angle and its alternate interior angle.

2. A triangle has angles $50^\circ$, $x$, and $2x$. Calculate the value of $x$.

3. An isosceles triangle has a vertex angle of $40^\circ$. What is the measure of the base angles?

Worksheet 3.3 & 3.4: Quadrilaterals & Polygons

1. In a parallelogram ABCD, angle A = 75°. State the size of angle B and angle C.

2. Calculate the sum of the interior angles of a Decagon (10 sides).

3. A regular polygon has an exterior angle of 24°. How many sides does the polygon have?

4. What is the size of ONE interior angle of a regular hexagon?

Worksheet 3.5: Circle Theorems

1. A triangle is drawn inside a circle where one side is the diameter. What is the angle opposite the diameter?

2. The angle subtended by an arc at the center of a circle is 130°. What is the angle subtended by the same arc at the circumference?

3. ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral. If angle A is 85°, calculate angle C (the opposite angle).

💯 Chapter 3 Evaluation (Used in Meeting 6)

Instructions: Answer all questions. Show all working steps. You must state the geometric reason for your calculations where asked. Total: 35 Marks.

Part A: Vocabulary & Basic Angles (10 Marks)

1. Which of the following quadrilaterals has exactly ONE pair of parallel sides? (1 mark)
A) Parallelogram    B) Rhombus    C) Trapezium    D) Kite

2. Find the unknown angle $x$ in a triangle with angles $45^\circ$, $82^\circ$, and $x^\circ$. (2 marks)

3. Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. Angle $a = 65^\circ$. What is the size of the co-interior angle next to it? (2 marks)

4. State the definition of an Isosceles Triangle regarding sides and angles. (3 marks)

5. Draw a circle. Label the following: Center, Radius, Chord, and Tangent. (2 marks)

Part B: Polygons & Quadrilaterals (12 Marks)

6. Calculate the sum of the interior angles of a Nonagon (9 sides). (3 marks)

7. A regular polygon has 12 sides.
a) Calculate the size of ONE exterior angle. (2 marks)
b) Calculate the size of ONE interior angle. (2 marks)

8. The angles of a quadrilateral are $x$, $2x$, $3x$, and $4x$. Calculate the value of $x$ and find the largest angle. (5 marks)

Part C: Circle Theorems & Construction (13 Marks)

9. Points A, B, and C lie on the circumference of a circle with center O. Angle AOB (at the center) is 140°. Find the size of angle ACB (at the circumference). (3 marks)

10. ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral. Angle $DAB = 110^\circ$ and Angle $ABC = 75^\circ$.
a) Find angle $BCD$. (2 marks)
b) Find angle $CDA$. (2 marks)

11. Construction: Using ONLY a compass, ruler, and pencil, construct a triangle with sides measuring 6 cm, 7 cm, and 9 cm. Leave your construction arcs visible! (6 marks)
