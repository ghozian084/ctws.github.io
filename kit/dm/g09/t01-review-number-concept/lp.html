Lesson Plan: Review of Number Concepts

School: SMP Semesta 2026/2025 | Grade: 9

Time Allocation: 9 Meetings (2 x 40 minutes = 80 mins per meeting)

📌 Topic: 1. Review of number concepts

Reference: Cambridge IGCSE® Math Core & Extended

Method: 5E (Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate)

Approach: Deep Learning & Culturally STEM

Character Focus: Critical Thinking, Creativity, Self-Regulated

Differentiation: Extension, Scaffolding, EAL, SEN

🎯 Objectives

  • C1.1, E1.1: Identify and use: natural numbers, integers, prime, square, cube numbers, common factors, common multiples, rational/irrational numbers, reciprocals.
  • C1.3: Calculate with squares, square roots, cubes, cube roots, other powers and roots.
  • C1.6, E1.6: Use four operations for calculations with integers, fractions and decimals, including negative numbers and correct ordering of operations.
  • C1.9, E1.9: Round values to a specified degree of accuracy.

📅 Meeting 1: Different Types of Numbers (Subtopic 1.1)

E1 ENGAGE | Hook & Prior Knowledge [10 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (4 min):

  • Display image of a traditional Indonesian market (Pasar). Ask: "If you buy 3 apples, pay with a Rp 50,000 note, and get change, what kind of numbers are you using? Are they natural? Integers?"
  • Students hold up mini whiteboards with their initial guesses.

Concept Introduction — Slide 2 (6 min):

  • Screen shows a chaotic cloud of numbers: \( -5, 0, \pi, \frac{3}{4}, \sqrt{2} \).
  • Ask: "Can we organize these into a system? What makes a number 'natural' versus 'unnatural'?"
E2 EXPLORE | Collaborative Categorization [20 minutes]
Collaborative Group Work
(Student-led)

Card Sorting Activity (15 min):

  • Distribute physical 'Number Card Set 1.1' to groups of 4.
  • Instruct groups to sort the cards into categories based on their own logic (no right or wrong yet).
  • Collaboration: Students must agree on category names and write them on sticky notes.

Gallery Walk (5 min):

  • Joyful Learning: Groups rotate to view one other group's desk. Ask: "Did they group \( \pi \) differently than you? Why?"
Scaffold students: SEN students receive Card Set 1.1A (fewer cards, visually distinct colors for negatives/fractions).
E3 EXPLAIN | Concept Consolidation [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Teacher-Facilitated Class Discussion (5 min):

  • Invite 2 groups to share their logic. Scribe findings on the board.
  • Bridge observation to concept: "The way you organized these matches how mathematicians classify the number system."

Direct Instruction — Slides 3–6 (15 min):

  • Slide 3: Natural vs Integers. Show Venn diagram (Natural inside Integers).
  • Slide 4: Rational Numbers. Define as \( a/b \). Ask: "Can 5 be written as a fraction? Show me on your whiteboards." (\( 5/1 \))
  • Slide 5: Irrational Numbers. Define as non-repeating, non-terminating. Examples: \( \pi, \sqrt{2}, \sqrt{3} \).
  • Slide 6: Mindful Check: "True or False: Every integer is a rational number, but not every rational number is an integer." Discuss with elbow partner.
Scaffold students: Refer to Slide 6B (Bilingual) for EAL students clarifying 'Terminating' vs 'Berhenti'.
E4 ELABORATE | Culturally STEM Application [20 minutes]
Independent Practice
(Student-paced)

Context Setting — Slide 7 (5 min):

  • Show dimensions of traditional Batik fabrics. Explain that weavers use precise rational measurements, but diagonal cuts often create irrational numbers.

Worksheet 1.1 Task (15 min):

  • Students independently classify real-world measurements: 1) Length \( 2.5 \)m (Rational/Irrational?), 2) Motif count of \( 150 \) (Natural/Integer?).
  • Self-Regulated: Students use the classroom wall chart to self-correct their logic before submitting.
Scaffold students: High achievers (Extension) must geometrically prove why the diagonal of a 1x1 Batik square is irrational (\( \sqrt{2} \)).
E5 EVALUATE | Formative Assessment [10 minutes]
Individual Task
(Teacher-monitored)

Exit Ticket — Slide 8 (10 min):

  • Hand out specific colored sticky notes.
  • Instruction: "Write one Rational number and one Irrational number. Place it on the door chart under the correct heading as you leave."
  • Teacher stands by door to instantly check for misconceptions.

📅 Meeting 2: Multiples and Factors (Subtopic 1.2)

E1 ENGAGE | Hook & Physical Modeling [10 minutes]
Whole Class Activity
(Teacher-led)

Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (4 min):

  • Play audio clip of a Javanese Gamelan. Ask: "The Gong strikes every 8 beats. The Kenong strikes every 6 beats. When do they strike together?"

Physical Simulation (6 min):

  • Half the class claps every 3 seconds. The other half taps desks every 4 seconds.
  • Ask: "At what second did we make a sound together? (12). This is a Common Multiple."
E2 EXPLORE | Visualizing Multiples [20 minutes]
Pair Work
(Student-led)

Number Grid Investigation (20 min):

  • Distribute 100-number grid printouts to pairs.
  • Instruct students to shade multiples of 6 in Yellow, and multiples of 8 in Blue.
  • Ask: "What numbers turn Green (overlap)? What is the smallest Green number? Write it down."
  • Critical Thinking: Ask pairs to define what the Green numbers represent without using the word "Multiple".
Scaffold students: SEN/Low students use a 1-50 grid to avoid cognitive overload.
E3 EXPLAIN | HCF and LCM Definitions [15 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–5 (15 min):

  • Slide 2: Define LCM (Lowest Common Multiple) using the grid data from E2.
  • Slide 3: Shift to Factors. Define as "numbers that fit perfectly into another."
  • Slide 4: Demonstrate listing factors systematically (Rainbow method) for 24 and 36.
  • Slide 5: Show the intersection of factors. Define HCF (Highest Common Factor).
  • Mindful Check: "Why do we look for the *Lowest* common multiple but the *Highest* common factor? Discuss."
E4 ELABORATE | Contextual Problem Solving [25 minutes]
Independent Practice
(Student-paced)

Application Task — Slide 6 (25 min):

  • Scenario: "You are preparing snack boxes for an Indonesian Independence Day event. You have 24 Kue Lemper and 36 Kue Pastel."
  • Students answer in books: 1) List all factors. 2) Find HCF. 3) What does the HCF mean here? (Maximum identical boxes).
  • Students complete Worksheet 1.2.
Scaffold students: Extension students receive a 3-variable problem (24 Lemper, 36 Pastel, 60 Risoles).
E5 EVALUATE | Exit Ticket [10 minutes]
Individual
(Teacher-monitored)

Concept Check — Slide 7 (10 min):

  • True or False: "The HCF of two numbers is always smaller than or equal to the LCM."
  • Students must write their answer and a 1-sentence mathematical justification on their exit slip.

📅 Meeting 3: Prime Numbers & Prime Factorization (Subtopic 1.3)

E1 ENGAGE | Defining Primes [10 minutes]
Interactive Q&A
(Teacher-led)

Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Display Borobudur Temple. "There are exactly 72 stupas. Let's break 72 down into its absolute smallest building blocks: Prime Numbers."

Whiteboard Check — Slide 2 (5 min):

  • Show numbers: 1, 2, 9, 15, 23. Ask students to write the prime numbers on mini whiteboards and hold them up.
  • Meaningful: Pause to address '1'. Ask: "Why is 1 NOT prime based on the exact definition of having exactly TWO distinct factors?"
E2 EXPLORE | Factor Trees [20 minutes]
Pair Work
(Student-led)

Factor Tree Activity (20 min):

  • Teacher models a Factor Tree for 60 on the board. Circle prime branches in Red.
  • Distribute A3 poster paper. Pairs construct factor trees for 72 and 100.
  • Joyful: Race to see which pair can find the prime factorization of 210 the fastest.
Scaffold students: Provide SEN students with a "Prime Number Bank" (2, 3, 5, 7, 11) printed at the top of their page.
E3 EXPLAIN | Venn Diagram Method [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 3–6 (20 min):

  • Slide 3: Writing primes in Index Notation (e.g., \( 2^3 \times 3^2 \)).
  • Slide 4: Why use Prime Factorization? (To find HCF/LCM of massive numbers easily).
  • Slide 5: The Venn Diagram Method. Walk through placing prime factors of 120 and 84 into intersecting circles.
  • Slide 6: Rule: "Intersection = HCF. Multiply ALL numbers in circles = LCM."
E4 ELABORATE | Calculation Practice [20 minutes]
Independent Task
(Student-paced)

Worksheet Task (20 min):

  • Write 150 as a product of prime factors in index form.
  • Use the Venn diagram method to find HCF and LCM of 45 and 60.
  • Self-Regulated: Students must check their LCM by doing standard listing if they finish early to prove the method works.
E5 EVALUATE | Exit Slip [10 minutes]
Individual Assessment

Quick Calculation — Slide 7 (10 min):

  • "Write down the prime factorization of 24 in index form."
  • Collect at the door.

📅 Meeting 4: Powers (Squares, Cubes, and Indices) (Subtopic 1.4)

E1 ENGAGE | Hook & Misconception Check [10 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (5 min):

  • Tell The Legend of Roro Jonggrang. "If Bandung Bondowoso started with 1 jin, and it doubled every hour (1, 2, 4, 8...), how do we write this quickly? Exponents!"

Misconception Check (5 min):

  • Ask: "Is \( 3^2 \) the same as \( 3 \times 2 \)?" Show fingers (1 for Yes, 2 for No).
  • Demonstrate on board: Draw a 3x3 grid (9) vs 3 groups of 2 (6).
E2 EXPLORE | Physical Modeling [20 minutes]
Group Work
(Student-led)

Cube Building Activity (20 min):

  • Hand out physical linking cubes to groups.
  • Instruct students to build physical representations of \( 2^2 \) (a square) and \( 2^3 \) (a cube).
  • Creativity: Ask groups to sketch what they think \( 2^4 \) looks like, realizing it moves beyond 3D space into abstract multiplication.
E3 EXPLAIN | Index Notation Rules [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Direct Instruction — Slides 2–5 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Vocabulary: Base vs Index/Power.
  • Slide 3: Calculate \( 5^3 \) step-by-step (\( 5 \times 5 \times 5 = 125 \)).
  • Slide 4: Negative Bases. Show \( (-2)^2 = 4 \), but \( (-2)^3 = -8 \).
  • Slide 5: Rule generation: Ask class, "What happens if the power is even vs odd on a negative base?"
Scaffold students: EAL refer to Slide 5B for terms 'Even/Genap' and 'Odd/Ganjil'.
E4 ELABORATE & E5 EVALUATE | Practice & Exit [30 minutes]
Independent Task
& Assessment

Application Task (20 min):

  • Calculate \( (-3)^4 \) and explain why it is positive.
  • Context: "A square rice plot has a side length of 15m. Find the area using powers."

Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Calculate \( (-5)^2 + 2^3 \). Show workings.

📅 Meeting 5: Roots (Square Roots, Cube Roots) (Subtopic 1.4)

E1 & E2 | Hook & Inverse Exploration [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Pair Work

Hook & Explore — Slides 1-2 (25 min):

  • "Yesterday we built squares. Today we are archaeologists. We found a square Kraton floor with 400 tiles. How many tiles on one side? We must 'un-build' it."
  • Write \( \sqrt{36} \) and \( \sqrt[3]{27} \) on the board. Pairs discuss trial-and-error methods to solve.
E3 EXPLAIN | Root Calculation Methods [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 3-6 (20 min):

  • Slide 3: Roots as the inverse of powers (Visual: Tree growing vs Roots).
  • Slide 4: Demonstrate using Prime Factorization to find exact roots of large numbers (e.g., \( \sqrt{144} \)).
  • Slide 5: Rational roots vs Surds (\( \sqrt{20} \)).
Scaffold students: Low achievers use their pre-made Prime Factor trees from M3 to find the roots.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Calculate \( \sqrt[3]{-64} \). (Class discussion: Can cube roots be negative? Yes).
  • Context: A water tank is a perfect cube with volume 1000 \( m^3 \). Find edge length.
  • Exit Ticket: Which is larger: \( \sqrt{81} \) or \( \sqrt[3]{125} \)?

📅 Meeting 6: Directed Numbers - Addition & Subtraction (Subtopic 1.5)

E1 ENGAGE | Physical Number Line [10 minutes]
Active Learning
(Teacher-led)

Teacher Hook & Action — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Display thermometer showing Puncak Jaya Wijaya at \( -5^\circ\text{C} \).
  • Ask students to stand up. "Take two steps forward (+2). Now take 5 steps backward (-5). Where are you relative to the start?"
E2 EXPLORE | Counter Sliding [15 minutes]
Individual
(Student-led)

Number Line Task (15 min):

  • Write \( 3 - 8 \) and \( -4 - 5 \) on the board.
  • Students use physical number lines on their desks, sliding a counter left and right to find the answers visually.
E3 EXPLAIN | Double Sign Rules [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 2-4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Explain rule for double signs: \( 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 \).
  • Slide 3: Metaphor: "Taking away a cold breeze makes the room warmer."
  • Slide 4: Guide 3 examples on the board, asking students to shout out the direction to move on the number line.
E4 & E5 | Application & Exit [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Calculate: \( -12 + 7 \) ; \( 8 - 15 \) ; \( -6 - (-9) \).
  • Exit Ticket: A scuba diver is at \( -15 \)m. They ascend \( 6 \)m. What is their new depth?
Scaffold students: Let SEN students continue using the physical sliding counter for the worksheet.

📅 Meeting 7: Directed Numbers - Mul/Div (Subtopics 1.5)

E1 & E2 | Hook & Pattern Discovery [25 minutes]
Teacher-led &
Pair Work

Hook & Explore — Slides 1-2 (25 min):

  • "If you have a debt (-10,000) and the bank 'forgives' (takes away) 3 debts, what happens? Let's decode multiplying negatives!"
  • Show pattern: \( 3 \times -2 = -6 \), \( 2 \times -2 = -4 \), \( 1 \times -2 = -2 \), \( 0 \times -2 = 0 \).
  • Pairs complete the pattern: What is \( -1 \times -2 = ? \) (Must be +2 based on pattern).
E3 EXPLAIN | Formalizing Rules [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 3-5 (20 min):

  • Slide 3: Formalize the rules: Same signs multiply/divide to positive. Different signs = negative.
  • Slide 4: Demonstrate calculating \( -5 \times (3 - 8) \) combining yesterday's skill with today's.
Scaffold students: Display a "Sign Rules Grid" permanently on the screen during practice.
E4 & E5 | Application & Evaluation [35 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (25 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Calculate: \( -6 \times -4 \) ; \( \frac{-24}{3} \) ; \( -4 \times 5 + 12 \).
  • Exit Ticket: Calculate \( \frac{-18}{-6} - 5 \).

📅 Meeting 8: Order of Operations (Subtopic 1.6)

E1 ENGAGE | The Vault Code [10 minutes]
Class Debate
(Teacher-led)

Teacher Hook — Slide 1 (10 min):

  • Write \( 50 - 2 \times 15 + 10 \) on the board WITHOUT brackets.
  • Ask half the class to calculate left-to-right, and the other half to use BODMAS. Compare the drastically different results (730 vs 30). "BODMAS is our safecracking manual."
E2 EXPLORE | Target 24 [20 minutes]
Collaborative
(Student-led)

Index Card Activity (20 min):

  • Hand out index cards with numbers and operation signs.
  • Students arrange them to create an expression that equals exactly 24, using at least one bracket.
E3 EXPLAIN | Complex Step-by-Step [20 minutes]
Direct Instruction

Direct Instruction — Slides 2-4 (20 min):

  • Slide 2: Complex expression: \( \frac{3 \times (12 - 4)^2}{2} \).
  • Slide 3: Model step-by-step resolution. Highlight the part being evaluated in each step with a different color ink.
Scaffold students: Extension students must insert brackets into \( 4 + 3 \times 2 - 1 = 13 \) to make it true.
E4 & E5 | Application & Spot the Error [30 minutes]
Independent Task

Elaborate (20 min) & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Evaluate: \( 15 - 3 \times (8 \div 2) \) ; \( 2^3 + \frac{20 - 5}{3} \).
  • Exit Ticket: Spot the Error. Provide a solved BODMAS equation with one deliberate mistake. Students circle the error and correct it.

📅 Meeting 9: Rounding & Chapter Evaluation (Subtopic 1.7)

E1 & E2 | Engage & Explain Rounding [25 minutes]
Direct Instruction
(Teacher-led)

Hook & Explain — Slides 1-3 (25 min):

  • "Sabang to Merauke is approx 5,245.83 km. Do we need exact decimal meters when planning a flight budget?"
  • Demonstrate rounding to Decimal Places (d.p.). "Look at the right neighbor. If it's 5 or more, round up!"
  • Explain Significant Figures (s.f.), emphasizing that leading zeros do not count.
  • Guided practice: Round \( 3.14159 \) to 2 d.p. and \( 0.0456 \) to 2 s.f.
E4 & E5 | Chapter Evaluation & Closure [55 minutes]
Summative Test
(Teacher-monitored)

Chapter 1 Test (45 min):

  • Remove visual aids. Distribute the Chapter 1 Evaluation covering all objectives.
  • Self-Regulated: Students pace themselves under exam conditions.

Closure & Exit Ticket (10 min):

  • Self-Reflection: On a slip of paper, rate confidence for Chapter 1 on a scale of 1-5, and write one specific skill mastered.

📝 Worksheets Compilation (For Practice & Homework)

Worksheet 1: Number Types, Factors & Primes

1. Categorize the following numbers into Integers, Primes, and Squares: \( -4, 7, 16, 0, 25, 31 \)

2. Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) and Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) of 36 and 48 using prime factorization.

3. Write down the reciprocal of \( \frac{3}{5} \) and the reciprocal of \( 7 \).

Worksheet 2: Powers and Roots

1. Evaluate the following: a) \( 4^3 \)   b) \( (-3)^2 \)   c) \( \sqrt{121} \)   d) \( \sqrt[3]{-27} \)

2. A square garden has an area of \( 225 \text{ m}^2 \). Find the length of one side.

3. Simplify the following expression: \( 2^4 + \sqrt{64} - 3^2 \).

Worksheet 3: Directed Numbers & Order of Operations

1. Calculate the following: a) \( -8 + 15 \)   b) \( -4 - (-12) \)   c) \( -6 \times -4 \)

2. Use the correct order of operations (BODMAS) to solve: \( \frac{24}{2^3} + (5 - 8)^2 \)

3. Insert brackets to make the following statement true: \( 5 + 2 \times 4 - 1 = 21 \)

💯 Chapter 1 Evaluation (Used in Meeting 9)

Instructions: Answer all questions. Show all working steps clearly. Total: 30 Marks.

Part A: Knowledge & Understanding (10 Marks)

1. Which of the following is an irrational number? (1 mark)
A) \( 0.333... \)    B) \( \sqrt{16} \)    C) \( \pi \)    D) \( -5 \)

2. Round the number \( 0.05682 \) to: a) 2 decimal places (2 marks) b) 2 significant figures (2 marks)

3. State whether True or False. If false, correct it. "The HCF of 12 and 18 is 36." (3 marks)

4. Write the reciprocal of \( -4 \). (2 marks)

Part B: Application & Calculation (20 Marks)

5. Calculate the exact value of: \( \sqrt[3]{125} \times 2^3 - \sqrt{81} \) (5 marks)

6. Evaluate using correct order of operations: \( \frac{3 \times (12 - -4)}{-2^2} \) (5 marks)

7. STEM Context: A marine biologist is tracking a submarine. It starts at sea level (\( 0 \text{ m} \)), dives down \( 450 \text{ m} \), then rises \( 120 \text{ m} \), and finally dives another \( 300 \text{ m} \).
a) Write a mathematical expression using directed numbers to represent this journey. (3 marks)
b) What is the final depth of the submarine? (2 marks)

8. A box holds a maximum of 14 kg. You have metal blocks that weigh \( 3^2 \) kg and \( 2^2 \) kg. Can you fit one of each into the box? Show your mathematical reasoning. (5 marks)
