Meeting 1: Types of Numbers (Part 1)

Main Concept The Number System

  • Natural: Counting numbers ($1, 2, 3\dots$)
  • Integer: Positive, negative, zero ($-2, 0, 5$)
  • Rational: Can be written as $a/b$. Includes stopping and repeating decimals ($0.25, 0.33\dots$)
  • Irrational: Non-stopping, non-repeating decimals ($\pi, \sqrt{2}$)

Activity Card Sort & Gallery Walk

Hook: "If you buy 3 apples, pay with Rp 50,000, and get change, what numbers are you using?"

Task: In your groups, take the Number Card Set. Sort them into categories that make sense to you. We will do a gallery walk to compare logic!

Meeting 2: Multiples & Factors (Part 1)

Main Concept HCF and LCM

  • Factors: Numbers that fit perfectly into another. Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) to split items equally.
  • Multiples: The "times tables" of a number. Find the Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) to see when events overlap.

Activity 100-Grid overlapping puzzle

Hook: "The Gong strikes every 8 beats. The Kenong strikes every 6 beats. When do they strike together?"

Task: Shade multiples of 6 in Yellow, and multiples of 8 in Blue on your grid. Where do they turn Green?

Meeting 3: Prime Numbers (Part 1)

Main Concept Prime Factorization

  • Prime Number: Exactly 2 factors (1 and itself). Note: 1 is NOT prime.
  • Factor Tree: Breaks a number down into primes.
  • Venn Diagram: Place primes inside. Intersection = HCF. Multiply all = LCM.

Activity Borobudur Building Blocks

Hook: "Borobudur has 72 stupas. Let's break 72 down into its absolute smallest building blocks."

Task: Work in pairs to create factor trees for 72 and 100 on large poster paper.

Meeting 4: Powers & Indices (Part 1)

Main Concept Base, Index, and Negatives

  • $3^4$ means $3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3$ (Base is 3, Index is 4).
  • Negative Bases:
    Even power = Positive answer (e.g., $(-2)^2 = 4$)
    Odd power = Negative answer (e.g., $(-2)^3 = -8$)

Activity Physical Modeling

Hook: "Bandung Bondowoso doubled his workforce every hour (1, 2, 4, 8...). How do we represent this?"

Task: Use physical linking cubes to build $2^2$ (square) and $2^3$ (cube). Try to sketch $2^4$!

Meeting 5: Roots (Part 1)

Main Concept Inverse Operations

  • Roots "un-build" squares and cubes. It asks: "What number multiplied by itself makes this?"
  • Exact roots are rational ($\sqrt{25} = 5$).
  • Surds are irrational ($\sqrt{20}$).

Activity The Archaeologist

Hook: "We found a square Kraton floor with an area of 400 tiles. How many tiles are on one side?"

Task: Discuss in pairs how to use prime factorization to find exact roots of large numbers without a calculator.

Meeting 6: Directed Numbers Add/Sub (Part 1)

Main Concept Double Signs

  • Adding a negative: $5 + (-3) \rightarrow 5 - 3$
  • Subtracting a negative: $5 - (-3) \rightarrow 5 + 3$ (Taking away cold makes it warmer!)

Meeting 7: Directed Numbers Mul/Div (Part 1)

Meeting 9: Rounding & Evaluation (Part 1)

Main Concept Rounding Rules

  • Decimal Places (d.p.): Count after the point.
  • Significant Figures (s.f.): Count from first non-zero.
  • Rule: Look at right neighbor. 5 or more = Round UP.
